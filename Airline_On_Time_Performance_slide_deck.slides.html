Airline On-Time Presentation¶

by Yusuf Abdulganiyu¶

Investigation Overview¶

In this investigation, I wanted to look at the characteristics of the major causes of delay for both arrival and departure. Also, to have a look at the monthly trend for the maximum delays duration over the years.

Dataset Overview¶

This dataset reports flights in the United States, including carriers, arrival and departure delays, and reasons for delays, from 2004 to 2008.

Distribution of Arrival delays¶

The arrival delay dataset takes on a range of values, between -40 and 300. Delays are values falling at a range greater than 0. Plotted on a hist plot, the distribution of arrival delays is unimodal and skewed to the right.

Distribution of Departure delays¶

Unlike the arrival delay, the departure delay dataset takes on range of values, between -25 and 250. Plotted on a hist plot, the distribution of departure delays is unimodal and also skewed to the right.

Departure delay vs Arrival delay¶

Plotting departure delay against arrival delay, taking a sample of n = 500, using a scatter plot, produces a linear positive relationship, of about 91% between arrival and departure delays.

Monthly trends of maximum delays duration¶

Plotting on the maximum delay duration, with respect to month for the study period of 2004 - 2008, it can be seen that both carrier and aircraft-related delays contribute more to overall delays duration as compared to others. It can be also observed that there is a large variation in delays duration from month to month. Delays due to security reasons are the least common with less variation.
